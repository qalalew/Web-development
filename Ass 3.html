Modern Music

Genres include Rock, Jazz, and Pop.

Rock
Emerged from rhythm and blues and country, featuring high-energy, guitar-driven sound.
Instruments: Electric guitar, electric bass, drum kit, and often keyboards/synthesizers.
Structure: Typically 3-4 chords for high-energy songs, or more for ballads.
Style: Strong backbeat, heavy energy, often focuses on social themes or rebellion.
Jazz
Rooted in blues, known for free-flowing, improvised, and instrumental focus.
Instruments: Saxophone, trumpet, piano, bass, and drums.
Structure: Complex harmonies, sophisticated chord progressions, and "passing chords".
Style: Syncopated rhythms, blue notes, and heavy use of improvisation (solos).
More Info

Pop Music (Popular Music): Designed for mainstream appeal, characterized by simplicity and hooky melodies.
Instruments: Varied, but often includes electronic production, synthesizers, and vocals.
Structure: Straightforward song structure (verse-chorus-verse), often using simple, repeated chord patterns.
Style: Softer, polished production, with intense focus on the vocalist.
